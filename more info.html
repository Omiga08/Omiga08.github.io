Heart Diseases

Coronary Artery Disease(CAD)

.Description:CAD occurs when the coronary arteries, which supply blood to the heart muscle, become narrowed or blocked due to plaque buildup.


.Symptoms:Chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, and fatigue.


.Risk Factors:High cholesterol, high blood pressure,smoking, diabetes, and a sedentary lifestyle


Heart Failure

.Description:. A condition where the heart is unable to pump blood effectively, leading to a buildup of fluid in the lungs and other parts of the body.


.Symptoms Shortness of breath, swelling in the legs and ankles, and fatigue.


.Risk Factors:High blood pressure, previous heart attack, and chronic conditions like diabetes.


Arrhythmias

.Description: Abnormal heart rhythms that can cause the heart to beat too fast, too slow, or irregularly.


.Symptoms: Palpitations, dizziness, fainting, and shortness of breath.


.Risk Factors:Heart disease, high blood pressure, smoking, and excessive alcohol or caffeine consumption.


Valvular Heart Disease

Description: Damage to one or more of the heart’s valves, affecting blood flow within the heart.

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, fatigue, and swelling in the ankles or feet.

Risk Factors: Age, infections, and congenital heart defects.

Heart Valve Disease

Description: Heart valve disease occurs when one or more of the heart’s valves don’t open or close properly. This can be due to infections, congenital defects, or other heart conditions.

symptoms:Shortness of breath,Fatigue,swelling in the ankles or feet or abdomen,chest pain and palpitations and fainting.

Risk Factorscongenital heart defects, High blood pressure, Diabetes and High cholesterol.

Cardiomyopathy

Description: Cardiomyopathy refers to diseases of the heart muscle that make it harder for the heart to pump blood. Causes can include genetic factors, long-term high blood pressure, or chronic rapid heart rate.

Symptoms:Shortness of breath,Fatigue, Palpitations, and chest pain

Risk Factors:Family history of cardiomyopathy,Long term high blood pressure and Chronic rapid heart rate

Main Diseases



Kidney Disease

.Description:Kidney disease refers to conditions that impair the function of the kidneys, which are responsible for filtering waste products and excess fluids from the blood. When the kidneys are damaged, waste products and fluids can build up in the body, leading to various health issues.


.Symptoms:Nausea and Vomiting,Loss of Appetite, Fatigue and weakness,sleep problems,Muscle Cramps and Swelling.


.Risk Factors:Diabetes,High blood Pressure, Heart Diseases, Family History and Obesity and Smoking and Age


Diabetes Disease

.Description:Diabetes mellitus is a group of diseases that affect how the body uses blood sugar (glucose). Glucose is vital for your health because it’s a significant source of energy for the cells that make up your muscles and tissues. Have main types are Type 1 Diabetes,Type 2 Diabetes,Gestational Diabetes and PreDiabetes


.SymptomsIncreased Thirst and Frequent Urination,Extreme Hunger,Unexplained Weight Loss,Fatigue and Blurred Vision. .


.Risk Factors: Family History,Weight,Inactivity, Age, and High Blood Pressure.


Stroke

.Description: A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting enough oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die within minutes. There are two main types of stroke:Ischemic Stroke and Hemorrhagic Stroke.


.Symptoms:Confusion,Vision Problems,Difficulty Walking and Severe Headache


.Risk Factors:High blood Pressure,Diabetes, Heart Diabetes, Obesity ,Smoking and High Cholesterol.


Lower Respiratory Infections

Description:Lower respiratory infections (LRIs) affect the airways and lungs, including conditions like bronchitis, pneumonia, and bronchiolitis. These infections can be caused by viruses, bacteria, or fungi. They are more severe than upper respiratory infections and can lead to significant health issues, especially in vulnerable populations such as the elderly, young children, and those with weakened immune systems.

Symptoms: Cough,Shortness of Breath,Chest Pain,Fever,Fatigue and Wheezing.

Risk Factors:Age,Smoking,Chronic Diseases,Weakened Immune System and Recent Respiratory Infection.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

Description:Chronic Bronchitis: This condition involves inflammation of the bronchial tubes, which carry air to and from the lungs.
Emphysema: This condition damages the alveoli (air sacs) in the lungs, reducing the surface area for gas exchange and causing shortness of breath.

symptoms:Shortness of Breath,Wheezing,Chest Tightness,Persistent Cough,Fatigue and Unintended Weight Loss.

Risk FactorsSmoking,Exposure to Lung Irritants,Genetics,Age and Respiratory Infections.

Infectious Diseases

Description:: Caused by pathogenic microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, parasites, or fungi. Examples include influenza, tuberculosis, and COVID-19.

Symptoms:Fever, chills, fatigue,muscle aches,coughing and sneezing.

Risk Factors:Smoking, air pollution, occupational hazards, and genetic factors can incrtease the risk of infections diseases.

Cancer Diseases



Carcinomas

.Description: are a type of cancer that begins in the epithelial cells, which line the inside and outside surfaces of the body. They are the most common type of cancer, accounting for 80-90% of all cancer diagnoses. Carcinomas can develop in various organs and tissues, including the skin, lungs, breasts, pancreas, and other organs. They are classified based on the type of epithelial cell in which they originate.common types are Basal Cell Carcinoma(BCC),Adenocarcinoma,Renal Cell Carcinoma(RCC) and Hepatocellular Carcinoma(HCC).


.Symptoms:Fatigue, Lump or area of thickening that can be felt under the skin,Weight changes,including unintended loss or gain and Skin changes, such as Yellowing, darkening or redness


.Risk Factors:Family history of cancer, Age(risk increases with age),Exposure to carcinogens(e.g,tobacco smoke, asbestos) and UV exposure from the sun or tanning beds


Leukemia

.Description: Cancer of the body’s blood-forming tissues, including the bone marrow and the lymphatic system.


.SymptomsFever or chills, persistent fatigue, frequent infections, easy bruising or bleeding.


.Risk Factors:Previous cancer treatment, genetic disorders (e.g., Down syndrome), exposure to certain chemicals, smoking.


Skin Cancer(Melanoma)

.Description: Cancer that develops in the melanocytes, the cells that produce melanin (pigment). Symptoms: New or unusual growths or changes in existing moles, sores that don’t heal.


.Symptoms:Confusion,Vision Problems,Difficulty Walking and Severe Headache


.Risk Factors: Excessive sun exposure, fair skin, history of sunburns, family history, use of tanning beds.


Pancreatic Cancer

Description:Cancer that begins in the tissues of the pancreas.

Symptoms: Jaundice, weight loss, loss of appetite, abdominal pain, blood clots.

Risk Factors:Smoking, diabetes, chronic pancreatitis, family history, obesity.

Central Nervous System

Description:Central Nervous System (CNS) cancers are a group of cancers that originate in the tissues of the brain and spinal cord. These cancers can be particularly challenging due to their location and the critical functions of the CNS. CNS cancers include both primary tumors, which originate in the brain or spinal cord, and secondary (metastatic) tumors, which spread to the CNS from other parts of the body. Primary CNS tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous).

symptoms:Headaches,Nausea and Vomiting, Vision Problems,Balance and Coordination Issues and Seizures.

Risk FactorsImmune System Disorders,Age, Family History,Radiation Exposure and Genetic Predisposition.

Sarcomas

Description:Sarcomas are a rare type of cancer that originate in the connective tissues of the body, such as bones, muscles, fat, blood vessels, nerves, and cartilage. They can occur anywhere in the body but are most commonly found in the arms, legs, and trunk. Two main types are Bone Sarcomas and Soft Tissue Sarcomas

Symptoms:Bone pain, Swelling, Fatigue, Weight loss, Abdominal pain and Difficulty breathing.

Risk Factors:Genetic Disorders,Radiation Exposure, Chemical Exposure ,Chronic Swelling and Family History.

Children Diseases



Common Cold

.Description:A viral illness common in young children.


.Symptoms:Fever, sores in the mouth, and a rash on the hands and feet.


.Risk Factors: Generally mild but can cause dehydration if mouth sores make swallowing difficult


Ear Infection(Otitis Media)

.Description: Infection of the middle ear, common in young children.


.SymptomsEar pain, difficulty hearing, fluid drainage from the ear, irritability


.Risk Factors::Can lead to hearing loss or speech delays if recurrent.


Chickenpox(Varicella)

.Description:A highly contagious viral infection causing an itchy rash and red spots. Can lead to bacterial infections of the skin, pneumonia, or encephalitis in severe cases.


.Symptoms:Itchy rash, fever, fatigue, loss of appetite.


.Risk Factors:Can lead to bacterial infections of the skin, pneumonia, or encephalitis in severe cases.


Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu)

Description: Inflammation of the stomach and intestines, usually due to a viral or bacterial infection.

Symptoms:Diarrhea, vomiting, stomach pain, fever.

Risk Factors:Dehydration is a major concern, especially in young children.

Respiratory Syncytial Virus(RSV)

Description:A common respiratory virus that can cause serious illness in infants and young children.

symptoms:Runny nose, decrease in appetite, coughing, sneezing, fever, wheezing.

Risk Factors Can lead to bronchiolitis or pneumonia, especially in infantsI

Scarlet Fever

Description: A bacterial illness that develops in some people who have strep throat.

Symptoms:Diarrhea, vomiting, stomach pain, fever

Risk Factors:Dehydration is a major concern, especially in young children.
